Tier 3

Clothing in this tier represents a more basic level of sustainability

Impact: Negligibly reduces environmental harm, and in some cases, could even contribute to environmental degradation. Choosing products from this tier is a missed opportunity to support more sustainable alternatives.

More intriguing information

Tier 3 (Red) clothing represents a more basic level of sustainability, with a higher environmental impact throughout its lifecycle. These garments typically last between 20-30 washes before showing noticeable wear, such as fading or fabric degradation. The manufacturing process in this tier often relies on conventional energy sources, which contribute to carbon emissions, and uses water-intensive production methods, putting additional strain on water resources. The materials used are primarily conventional, such as polyester, nylon, or non-organic cotton, which have a higher environmental footprint due to the use of synthetic fibers and chemical-intensive agricultural practices. These materials also shed microplastics during washing, contributing to ocean pollution. Additionally, labor practices in Tier 3 production may not always meet ethical standards, affecting worker conditions and pay.

Packaging for Tier 3 products is often made from plastic or other non-recyclable materials, adding to the global waste problem. At the end of their life, these garments are typically not recyclable or biodegradable, meaning they are more likely to end up in landfills, where they take years to break down, leaching harmful chemicals into the environment. While Tier 3 clothing may be more affordable and accessible, its environmental cost is significant. It is suitable for consumers just starting to make more sustainable choices, but it remains a less eco-friendly option when compared to higher-tier alternatives, which offer better durability, ethical production practices, and minimal environmental impact.

The pros of using eco tier

Using Eco Tier offers numerous benefits for both consumers and brands. It provides clear, easy-to-understand sustainability icons that help consumers make informed, eco-friendly shopping choices, promoting conscious consumerism. This transparency fosters a culture of responsibility, making it simpler to identify products with a lower environmental impact, reducing waste, carbon emissions, and other environmental harms. For brands, Eco Tier supports and highlights sustainable practices, helping them stand out in a competitive market and meet growing consumer demand for transparency.

The simple integration of Eco Tier into retail systems encourages more widespread adoption of sustainable practices, driving industry-wide change. By guiding consumers toward greener choices, Eco Tier contributes to long-term global goals like net-zero emissions and a circular economy, where resources are conserved, waste is minimized, and sustainability becomes second nature.
